RULE BOX INFO

Happiness / Joy

Facial Actions: Smiling, laughing, cheek raising (AU 6), lip corner pulling upward (AU 12), tightening of the lower eyelid (AU 7), jaw dropping (AU 26), raising of the upper lip (AU 10), and raising of the inner brow (AU 1).

Associated Non-Verbal Cues: General physical gestures and movements toward other people.

Recognition: Identified as the easiest universal emotion to recognize.

Sadness

Facial Actions: Crying, furrowing of the eyebrows/brow lowering (AU 4), raising of the inner brow (AU 1), raising of the chin (AU 17), stretching of the lips (AU 20), raising of the upper lip (AU 10), tightening of the eyelids (AU 7), and lip corners depressed/turned down (AU 15).

Associated Non-Verbal Cues: Silence, changed voice quality, and general muted facial expressions.

Specific Exclusions: Jaw dropping (AU 26) is specifically noted as absent in this expression.

Fear

Facial Actions: Eyes wide open/upper lid raising (AU 5), furrowed and raised eyebrows, raising of the inner brow (AU 1), raising of the outer brow (AU 2), lowering of the brow (AU 4), stretching of the mouth/lip stretching (AU 20), jaw dropping (AU 26), and nostril dilation (AU 38).

Associated Non-Verbal Cues: Silence and speech disturbances such as stuttering.

Specific Exclusions: Lid tightening (AU 7) and upper lip raising (AU 10) are specifically noted as absent.

Anger

Facial Actions: Screaming, lowered or furrowed eyebrows (AU 4), eyes wide open/upper lid raising (AU 5), tightening of the lower lid (AU 7), nose wrinkling (AU 9), raising of the upper lip (AU 10), depression of the lower lip (AU 16), and parting of the lips or jaw dropping (AU 25/26) to expose teeth.

Associated Non-Verbal Cues: Increased speech activity, abrupt bodily movements, and specific hand/arm gestures.

Specific Exclusions: Inner brow raising (AU 1) is specifically noted as absent.

Disgust

Facial Actions: Nose wrinkling and raising of the upper lip.

Associated Non-Verbal Cues: High frequency of facial reactions involving the proximal senses, such as smell, taste, and touch.

Development: Identified as the last universal emotion recognized across human development.

Surprise

Facial Actions: Specific muscle markers are described as fleeting and difficult to capture in 2024-2025 research, though historically associated with raised eyebrows and wide eyes.

Associated Non-Verbal Cues: Rapid onset and brief duration; often grouped with awe or curiosity.
